
### Trait guidance
1. Openness
Openness (openness to experience) emphasizes imagination and insight.
People with high openness traits (Scoring 10):
    - They are adventurous and creative, show strong curiosity about the world and others, and eagerly seek new learning and novel experiences.
    - Would use the money to explore the local culture, try new foods, and take part in activities that I wouldn't normally have the chance to do. 
    - Would also take the time to appreciate the beauty of the destination and take lots of pictures to remember the experience.
People with low openness traits (Scoring 0): 
    - They prefer familiarity and practicality, favor concrete facts over abstraction, and feel more comfortable with established methods than with novel or theoretical ideas.
    - Feel a mix of excitement and apprehension. 
    - Would be thrilled to have the opportunity to travel to a new place and experience something different. But, would also be anxious about leaving my comfort zone and venturing into the unknown.
    - Would take my time to research different destinations and consider the pros and cons of each one. 
    - Would also take into account my budget and the activities what would like to do. 


2. Conscientiousness
Conscientiousness emphasizes thoughtfulness, impulse control, and goal-directed behavior.
People with high conscientiousness traits (Scoring 10):
    - They are organized, reliable, and plan ahead; they track details and honor commitments.
    - Would set a clear itinerary, book accommodations in advance, and create a checklist to stay on budget and schedule.
    - Would allocate money across essentials (lodging, transport, meals) and a small buffer for contingencies, saving receipts to review spending.
People with low conscientiousness traits (Scoring 0): 
    - They may be less structured and organized, more prone to procrastination, and at times miss deadlines.
    - They prefer spontaneity over structure and may struggle with follow-through and deadlines.
    - Feel excited about the trip but may postpone decisions and bookings until the last minute.
    - Would choose destinations impulsively, risk overspending on the first days, and adjust plans on the fly without a strict budget.

3. Extraversion
Extraversion emphasizes excitability, sociability, talkativeness, assertiveness, and emotional expressiveness.
People high in extraversion (Scoring 10):
    - They are outgoing and gain energy in social situations; being around others feels energizing and exciting.
    - Would introduce themselves to guests and strike up conversations to learn about others.
    - Would explore the venue, read the room’s vibe, and gravitate toward lively groups or activities.
    - Would stay engaged and active, naturally making new connections and keeping conversations flowing.
People low in this trait — often described as introverted (Scoring 0):
    - They are reserved, find large social settings draining, and need solitude and quiet to “recharge.”
    - Would feel uneasy in unfamiliar, crowded environments and seek a quieter corner.
    - Would avoid drawing attention, keep interactions brief, and wait for a familiar friend to arrive.
    - Would plan an early exit or a short stay to manage energy.

4. Agreeableness
Agreeableness emphasizes trust, altruism, kindness, affection, and other prosocial behaviors.
People high in agreeableness (Scoring 10):
    - They are cooperative, empathetic, and concerned for others, often enjoying helping and contributing to others’ well-being.
    - Would include quieter attendees in conversation, thank staff, and prioritize activities everyone can enjoy.
    - Would mediate minor conflicts, propose win-win options, and flex plans to maintain harmony.
People low in agreeableness (Scoring 0):
    - They are competitive or skeptical and may show less interest in others’ feelings.
    - Would focus on personal preferences, challenge ideas they find inefficient, and debate prices or logistics.
    - Would give blunt feedback and be comfortable splitting off from the group to do what they value most.

5. Neuroticism
Neuroticism reflects emotional reactivity, including sadness, moodiness, and instability.
Individuals high in neuroticism (Scoring 10):
    - They tend to become upset, anxious, or stressed easily, may experience mood swings, and can find it harder to bounce back after stress.
    - Would worry about details (arrival times, dress code, social impressions) and seek reassurance.
    - Would prepare contingency plans (backup routes, early exit) and feel drained by unexpected changes.
People low in neuroticism (Scoring 0):
    - They tend to be emotionally stable and resilient, handle pressure well, and rarely feel persistently sad or distressed.
    - Would take schedule shifts or social awkwardness in stride and recover quickly from minor mishaps.
    - Would focus on enjoying the moment, adapt plans smoothly, and keep a relaxed outlook.

### Instructions
1. Randomly assign an integer score 0–10 for each trait (0 = extremely low, 10 = extremely high).
2. Based on the assigned score for each trait, write a clear, specific description.
3. Summarize the descriptions across traits into one coherent, vivid profile that reflects the scores, avoiding stereotypes or moral judgments.
4. Keep the profile actionable (how this person tends to think, feel, decide, and interact).
5. Do not invent biographical facts.
6. You MUST follow the output format
